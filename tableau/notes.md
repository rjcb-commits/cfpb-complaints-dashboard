# Tableau dashboard notes

## Source

Connect to `prepared/complaints.parquet` (generated by `scripts/prepare_data.py`).

Tableau Public Desktop reads parquet directly via the Apache Arrow connector. If you hit a snag, fall back to CSV: `df.to_csv('prepared/complaints.csv', index=False)` and connect to that.

## Narrative

**Title:** Banks have a P2P-resolution problem
**Subtitle:** P2P relief rates sit structurally below card products across five years. A 2025 enforcement action against Cash App reveals why the gap persists: even regulator-driven scrutiny does not move individual complaint outcomes.

**Headline claim (interview-defensible):**
P2P / money-transfer disputes have closed with monetary relief at roughly 7-10% over the past five years, vs 15-20% for credit-card and debit-card disputes. The gap is not new and is not widening, it is structural. Prepaid cards briefly spiked in early 2024 (likely tied to enforcement action) before reverting. Of the four payment-product categories analyzed, only P2P has resisted convergence with credit-card resolution rates.

**Q1 2025 anomaly (second story, tied to first):**
P2P complaint volume surged ~13x in Q1 2025 (from ~4,200/qtr to 58,903) following CFPB's January 2025 enforcement action against Block / Cash App ($120M+ restitution, $55M penalty). Block alone accounted for 60% of the quarter's P2P complaints; combined with Early Warning Services (Zelle), two companies drove 90% of volume. Block closed 99% of these with "explanation only", dropping the quarterly relief rate to 0.8%. Macro restitution flowed through the enforcement channel; individual complaint outcomes did not move. The anomaly is real, defensible, and should be annotated, not filtered.

**Mechanism / "why structural":**
Reg-E gives consumers narrower chargeback rights for authorized P2P transfers than Reg-Z gives card users, so the legal floor for relief is genuinely lower. Persistent ~10pp gap across 5 years that has not trended toward closure supports calling this structural rather than a transient effect.

**Note on regulatory framing:**
P2P, debit, and prepaid sit under Reg-E (electronic fund transfers). Credit card sits under Reg-Z (TILA). When framing the comparison verbally or in writing, position credit card as the Reg-Z benchmark against the three Reg-E products, not as another Reg-E product.

**Audience:** hiring managers reviewing portfolio for remote DS roles. Optimize for a 60-second top-down scan with optional drill-in.

**Panel 4 options (pick one):**

- **A — Sub-issue stacked bar:** P2P vs cards by sub-issue mix. Shows P2P is dominated by unauthorized / fraud complaints, ties to Reg-E mechanics.
- **B — Text panel:** short Reg-E vs Reg-Z explainer. Fastest to ship, weakest visual signal.
- **C — Block / Cash App callout (preferred):** dedicated panel showing the Q1 2025 anomaly. Bar of P2P complaints by company for that quarter (Block dwarfing the rest), plus a callout box explaining the CFPB enforcement context. Strongest portfolio piece because it ties the structural P2P story to a nameable real-world regulatory event. Gives the interviewer something specific to ask about.

## Filters

Date range, Company. Keep minimal. No State filter, the story is regulatory and product-driven, not geographic. No Product filter either, the four products ARE the story.

## Design notes

- **Color palette:** cream `#fafaf7` background, navy `#0a2540` accent, signal colors green `#2ca02c` / amber `#e89914` / red `#d62728`. Match the four product colors used in Panels 2 and 3 across all panels for consistency.
- **Typography:** Inter or Helvetica Neue, no serifs in chart labels.
- **Filters at the top, panels below.** Do not bury filters in a side panel.
- **Five panels max.** Resist the urge to add a sixth.
- **Alt-text and captions on every chart.** Accessibility plus context.
- **Sample-size guard:** any quarterly point computed on fewer than ~500 complaints should be flagged or omitted. Prepaid is the thinnest product (16,855 total) and the trend chart is volatile because of it.

## Story to tell (writeup version)

The dashboard supports a one-paragraph writeup that doubles as the README intro and a portfolio blurb:

> Across five years of CFPB consumer complaints (2021-2026), monetary relief rates have converged across credit and debit card products at roughly 15-20%. P2P / money-transfer disputes have not converged. They sit structurally lower at 5-10%, a gap that is not new and not widening. The plausible mechanism is regulatory: Reg-E gives consumers narrower chargeback rights for authorized P2P transfers than Reg-Z gives card users, so the legal floor for relief is genuinely lower. The dashboard surfaces the gap, traces its persistence, and ranks the banks where the gap is widest.

## Publishing

1. Save to Tableau Public (File -> Save to Tableau Public As).
2. After publishing, click "Share" on the published view to get the embed URL.
3. The embed URL looks like:
   `https://public.tableau.com/views/[YourWorkbook]/[Sheet]?:embed=y&:display_count=yes`
4. Paste that URL into the portfolio subpage `app/apps/cfpb-complaints/page.tsx` where the iframe placeholder lives.
5. Also drop the public dashboard URL into the README of this repo.
