# Tableau dashboard notes

## Source

Connect to `prepared/complaints.parquet` (generated by `scripts/prepare_data.py`).

Tableau Public Desktop reads parquet directly via the Apache Arrow connector. If you hit a snag, fall back to CSV: `df.to_csv('prepared/complaints.csv', index=False)` and connect to that.

## Design notes

- **Color palette:** match the portfolio (cream `#fafaf7` background, navy `#0a2540` accent, green `#2ca02c` / amber `#e89914` / red `#d62728` for trend signal). Avoid Tableau's default rainbow.
- **Typography:** Inter or Helvetica Neue, no serifs in chart labels.
- **Filters at the top, panels below.** Don't bury filters in a side panel — visitors need to see them immediately.
- **No more than 5 panels.** Pick the strongest five. Resist the urge to add a 6th.
- **Alt-text and captions on every chart** — accessibility plus context.
- **No dollar signs or commas in axis labels for counts** — Tableau defaults to `1,234,567`, which is fine.

## Story to tell

The headline of the writeup is "what crosses my desk vs what escalates publicly." The dashboard should support that narrative. Specifically:

1. The volume-over-time panel shows that EFT-related complaints (the Reg-E adjacent ones) have grown 3x since 2018 — that's the trend justifying why this kind of modeling work is in demand.
2. The top-issues panel surfaces *which* sub-issue patterns dominate — unauthorized transactions, deposit/withdrawal disputes, etc.
3. The resolution panel shows that bigger banks settle a higher fraction with monetary relief than smaller ones — believable but worth showing.

## Publishing

1. Save to Tableau Public (File → Save to Tableau Public As).
2. After publishing, click "Share" on the published view to get the embed URL.
3. The embed URL looks like:
   `https://public.tableau.com/views/[YourWorkbook]/[Sheet]?:embed=y&:display_count=yes`
4. Paste that URL into the portfolio subpage `app/apps/cfpb-complaints/page.tsx` where the iframe placeholder lives.
5. Also drop the public dashboard URL into the README of this repo.

## Once stable

Save a packaged workbook (`.twbx`) to `tableau/cfpb-complaints.twbx` and commit it. That's a portable copy anyone could open without your data files.
